Unique Name in Repairing Servo, Step and Reluctance

A servo motor is designed for high-precision control of angular or linear position, speed, and torque, often used in robotics and automation systems. It typically includes a built-in encoder or sensor for feedback, enabling closed-loop control to adjust its position accurately. Servo motors offer smooth operation at various speeds and are ideal for tasks requiring fine control, like CNC machines or robotic arms.,

A stepper motor, on the other hand, moves in fixed, discrete steps, making it ideal for applications requiring precise, incremental movement, such as 3D printers or camera platforms. It operates in an open-loop system, without needing feedback, and can maintain position even when powered off.

A reluctance motor works on the principle of magnetic reluctance, where the rotor aligns with the lowest reluctance path in the stator’s magnetic field. They are known for their simple design and efficiency. Without using permanent magnets or windings on the rotor, reluctance motors are cost-effective and often used in industrial drives where efficiency and durability are prioritized.
